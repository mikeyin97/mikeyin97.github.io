Welcome to my website.

My name is Michael and I'm currently a third-year student at the University of Toronto, majoring in Engineering Science. I love programming, and I often participate in hackathons or engage in my personal projects. My fields of interest mainly include data science, machine learning, and computer vision.

Outside of programming, I enjoy playing basketball and soccer, listening to music, taking walks, playing video games, and reading.

I'm always looking for new opportunities. You can find my resume here.

  • May 2016 - August 2016 // Product Development Researcher
    Intelligent Sensory Microsystems Lab
    Toronto, Ontario

    • Analyzed sensor data using machine learning algorithms such as k-means and fuzzy c-means clustering in Python.
    • Developed a web framework to share data visualizations online using node.js.

  • September 2016 - May 2017 // Hardware Technologist
    IEEE UofT
    Toronto, Ontario

    • Helped build, design, test and provide feedback on various circuits.
    • Mentored workshops and events.

  • May 2017 - August 2017 // Computer Science Researcher
    Dynamic Graphics Project
    Toronto, Ontario

    • Used the OpenCV C++ library to calibrate and debug cameras and projectors.
    • Implemented image projection and analysis algorithms to differentiate ambient light from direct light.
    • Developed structured light algorithms to retrieve 3D depth information of objects in a scene.
