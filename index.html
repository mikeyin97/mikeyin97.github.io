Hello, I'm Ji Tong (Michael) Yin!

I'm a 3rd-year PhD candidate at UBC in Human-Computer Interaction

My primary research interest is in understanding and supporting technology-mediated interpersonal and intrapersonal social relationships.

On the interpersonal side, I am interested in how people interact with each other, as well as with virtual agents, in digital environments such as social media, livestreaming, video games, and social VR. On the intrapersonal side, I focus on how people construct self-reflection and "conversations with themselves," mediated through technology use in their daily lives.

I am currently supervised by Professor Robert Xiao at the UBC X-Lab, with whom I also did my Master's. Previously, I completed my undergraduate studies in engineering science at UofT. In my free time, I enjoy playing badminton and running. If you are interested in learning more about my research or my academic journey or if you just want to chat, feel free to send me a message on any of my socials!
